Part A: Introduction

The Carbon Credit Market plays a crucial role in global efforts to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and combat climate change. This market is divided into two main segments: the mandatory carbon credit market and the voluntary carbon credit market.

Mandatory Carbon Credit Market

The mandatory carbon credit market is regulated by governments and international organizations. Participating companies must comply with emission quotas set by regulatory agencies. If a company's emissions exceed the quota, they must purchase additional carbon credits from the market. A prime example is the European Union Emissions Trading System (EU ETS), which accounts for approximately 90% of the global carbon credit market value. In 2021, the EU ETS was valued at around 760 billion EUR (approximately 899 billion USD), with a growth rate of 164% compared to 2020.

Voluntary Carbon Credit Market

The voluntary carbon credit market allows individuals and companies to purchase carbon credits to offset their emissions without being legally required to do so. These credits are typically issued by environmental projects such as reforestation, forest conservation, and other emission reduction activities. This market reached a value of approximately 2 billion USD in 2021, an increase of 282% compared to 2020. Voluntary carbon credits are usually verified by non-governmental organizations such as Verra and Gold Standard.

Part B: Market Overview

Market Potential: The carbon credit market is experiencing strong growth. The EU ETS is not only the largest carbon trading system in the world but also one of the fastest-growing markets. In 2021, the price of an EUA credit increased from around 25 EUR to over 60 EUR, indicating strong demand and value growth. The voluntary market is rapidly increasing as businesses and individuals seek to offset their emissions. In 2021, the price of voluntary credits increased from 1-3 USD to 15-20 USD per credit, reflecting significant market growth.

Market Challenges

Despite its potential, the carbon credit market faces several challenges: Emission Reduction Measurement and Verification is a complex and costly process, especially for voluntary carbon credit projects. The lack of standardization and transparency in this process leads to doubts about the accuracy of the credits. Voluntary Credit Trading lacks a centralized and standardized trading infrastructure, making it difficult to trade and verify credits. This also reduces the market's liquidity. Cost and Barriers to Entry are significant for many small businesses and individuals.
